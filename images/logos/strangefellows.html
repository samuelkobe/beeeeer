    Malevolent or merely mischievous, Yokai are shape-shifting supernatural demons of Japanese folklore and thought to be unlucky. On the last day of winter, handfuls of roasted soybeans are thrown at masked demons to drive away bad luck for the year to come. Once the evil spirits have been exorcised, each member of the family eats one roasted soybeans for each year of their life, plus one to bring good health and fortune in the coming year.

    If you suspect your tea kettle may be a Yokai, or that there is a shape-shifting dog following you, have a glass of our Japanese-inspired beer to exorcise your demons and bring good luck for the year ahead.

    Guizer

    Milk Stout

    Puffs of breath mist the cold air as a lone horn signals to the crowd of hairy, torch-bearing men.  Old ballads @the sound of thousands of stamping feet accompany a massive viking longship as its dragged through the darkened streets.  The procession ends with the torching of the longship, symbolizing the rebirth of the sun.  Followed by uproarious celebration until the wee hours, this pagan festival marks the end of the Yuletide feasting, and honours Shetlanders Norse ancestry.

    We haven’t got a longship to burn, but we can still honour our inner viking with a glass of our Northern-style Milk Stout.  Smooth and sessionable, this ale gets its soft background of sweetness from lactose, that will leave proclaiming “Drink hail, I drink & good luck be to you.”
